# WhiteBoardDefense -- Student Guide

## What is this?

WhiteBoardDefense is an online oral defense tool. Your instructor will use it to
ask you questions about a paper, project, or presentation you submitted, and
you'll answer using a mix of writing, sketching, and short verbal-style typed
explanations. It helps confirm you understand and can explain your own work.

---

## 1. Joining a session

Your instructor will send you a **link** (or a QR code). It looks something like:

```
https://203.0.113.45:3456/?sessionId=ABC123&role=student
```

Click the link (or scan the QR code) on the device you'll use for the defense --
a laptop or tablet with a decent-sized screen works best, since you'll be
sketching diagrams.

### You'll see a security warning -- that's expected

Because this server uses a security certificate that isn't from a public
certificate authority, your browser will show a warning like **"Your connection
is not private"** or **"Not secure."** This is expected and does not mean
anything is wrong. Click:

- **Chrome/Edge:** "Advanced" -> "Proceed to [site] (unsafe)"
- **Firefox:** "Advanced..." -> "Accept the Risk and Continue"
- **Safari:** "Show Details" -> "visit this website"

You only need to do this once per browser/device.

---

## 2. During the defense

The session moves through a few phases:

1. **Review** -- your instructor sets up the questions ahead of time; you may
   not see this part.
2. **Live Board** -- you'll be given diagram/whiteboard questions. Use the
   drawing or diagram-building tools provided to sketch your answer.
3. **Oral Examination** -- an AI-assisted examiner will ask follow-up questions
   in a chat window based on what you drew or wrote. Answer in your own words,
   in a few sentences -- this isn't a place to paste in pre-written or
   AI-generated answers, since the system is specifically designed to notice
   that and will prompt you to explain more naturally if it happens.
4. **Final Evaluation** -- your instructor reviews (and may adjust) the final
   scorecard.

### Tips
- Answer as if you were explaining the concept out loud to your instructor --
  clear and in your own words, not overly formal or padded.
- If you're asked to "explain that more naturally," it's not a penalty by
  itself -- just answer the follow-up honestly in your own words and move on.
- If your connection drops or you accidentally refresh the page, the session
  will pick back up where you left off in most cases -- don't panic, just
  reopen the link.

---

## 3. If something goes wrong

| Problem | What to do |
|---|---|
| The page won't load at all | Double-check you copied the whole link, including `https://` and the port number after the colon. Confirm you have an internet connection. |
| "Site can't be reached" | The server might be temporarily down -- let your instructor know. |
| I refreshed and lost my progress | Reopen the same link -- it should restore your session. If it doesn't, tell your instructor right away. |
| The whiteboard/diagram tool won't respond | Try refreshing once (see above -- your progress should be saved) or switching to a different browser (Chrome or Edge recommended). |

If none of that works, contact your instructor directly -- don't spend the
whole session troubleshooting on your own.
